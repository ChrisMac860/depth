Help

What DEPTH does

DEPTH compares two groups of numbers, a control group and a treatment group, across their entire distribution, rather than at the average alone. Instead of collapsing each group to a single mean and running a t-test, it estimates how the gap between the groups changes at every point in the range, from the smallest values to the largest.

Reading the chart

X-axis
Control quantile, from 0 (the smallest control value) to 1 (the largest).
Top axis
A secondary axis along the top shows the approximate control value at that quantile, so you can read the x-axis in the original units as well as in quantile terms.
Line
The line is the estimated shift, Δ (treatment minus control), at each quantile.
Shaded band
A simultaneous 95% (1−α) confidence band. The band is valid across the whole curve at once, not only point by point, so the entire shape shown holds at that confidence level.
Dashed line
Zero: no difference between the groups.
Warm/red regions
Quantile ranges where the band excludes zero: these are the statistically significant ranges.
Missing band edge
Where a band edge is missing at the extremes (near quantile 0 or 1), the band is unbounded there: this is a small-sample-tail effect, not a data error.

The two methods

KS band (default)
Exact and distribution-free: it inverts the two-sample Kolmogorov–Smirnov acceptance region (Doksum & Sievers, 1976) and is fast to compute.
Bootstrap
A quantile-domain sup-t band built by resampling both groups (2000 draws by default), evaluated on a 5%–95% quantile grid. It is slower than the KS band, but useful as a cross-check.

α (significance)

α sets the confidence level of the band: the band shown gives simultaneous 1−α coverage. A sensible range is 0.01–0.10; the default is 0.05 (a 95% band). A smaller α widens the band and makes significance harder to find; a larger α narrows it.

The stats row

n, m
Sample sizes of the control and treatment groups.
mean diff
The difference between the group averages, which is what a t-test evaluates.
median shift
Difference of the group medians.
welch p
The p-value from Welch's t-test, a mean-only test shown for contrast.
ks p
The p-value from the two-sample Kolmogorov–Smirnov test, which is sensitive to any difference in distribution, not only a shift in the mean.
verdict chip
Whether the confidence band excludes zero anywhere along the curve.

The three example datasets

Restaurant tips (small)
Seaborn's tips.csv. Dinner vs Lunch tip amounts. n=176 (Dinner, control) / m=68 (Lunch, treatment).
NYC taxi fares (medium)
Seaborn's taxis.csv. Credit-card vs cash fares. Tips were not used, because cash tips are recorded as 0 in the source data; 44 rows with a null payment type were dropped.
Diamond prices (large)
Seaborn's diamonds.csv. Ideal vs Premium cut prices. n=21,551 (Ideal, control) / m=13,791 (Premium, treatment).

Bringing your own data

Paste or upload data in one of two formats. Each group needs at least 10 values. Data goes to the local backend only; nothing leaves your machine.

Two columns
Two delimiter-separated numeric columns (comma, tab, or semicolon; a header row is tolerated). Column mode pairs values row by row, so rows missing either value are skipped. If your two groups have different sizes, use the two-block format instead.
Two blocks
Two blocks of numbers separated by a blank line. The two blocks may have different sizes.

Editing the chart

Click the chart title, subtitle, or either axis label to edit it in place. Type the new text; Enter or clicking away commits the change, Escape cancels it. Edited text becomes the source of truth for that chart and carries through to the downloaded report.

Download report

Produces one self-contained .html file: the chart with your edited labels, a stats table, the list of significant regions, the dataset's provenance, and a generated-on timestamp. It opens offline in any browser and is safe to email or archive; it makes no external requests.
